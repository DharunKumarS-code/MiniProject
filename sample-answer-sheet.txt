Q1. What is the primary function of chlorophyll in plants?
Answer: B) Photosynthesis

Q2. Explain the process of photosynthesis in 2-3 sentences.
Answer: Photosynthesis is the process by which plants use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to produce oxygen and energy in the form of glucose. It takes place in the chloroplasts of plant cells. The light energy is absorbed by chlorophyll and used to convert CO2 and water into glucose and oxygen.

Q3. Which organelle is responsible for photosynthesis?
Answer: C) Chloroplast

Q4. What are the two main stages of photosynthesis?
Answer: The two main stages are the light-dependent reactions and the light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle).

Q5. What gas is released as a byproduct of photosynthesis?
Answer: Oxygen (O2)

Q6. Write the chemical equation for photosynthesis.
Answer: 6CO2 + 6H2O + light energy -> C6H12O6 + 6O2

Q7. What is the role of sunlight in photosynthesis?
Answer: Sunlight provides the energy needed to split water molecules and drive the chemical reactions that produce glucose.

Q8. Where does the light-dependent reaction occur?
Answer: In the thylakoid membranes of the chloroplast.

Q9. What molecule carries energy in the Calvin cycle?
Answer: ATP and NADPH

Q10. Discuss the importance of photosynthesis in the ecosystem.
Answer: Photosynthesis is the foundation of most food chains on Earth. It produces oxygen that all aerobic organisms need to survive. It also removes carbon dioxide from the atmosphere, helping to regulate climate. Without photosynthesis, life as we know it would not exist on Earth.
